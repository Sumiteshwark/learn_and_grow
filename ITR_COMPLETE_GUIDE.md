## 1. Foundational Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Financial Year (FY)** | The year in which you **earn** income. Runs **1 April → 31 March** (e.g., FY 2025‑26). Also called the *Previous Year*. |
| **Assessment Year (AY)** | The year **immediately after** the FY, in which that income is assessed and the ITR is filed (FY 2025‑26 → AY 2026‑27). |
| **Assessee** | Any person liable to pay tax or file a return under the Income Tax Act. |
| **PAN** | Permanent Account Number — 10‑character ID; the primary key for all tax records. |
| **Aadhaar** | 12‑digit UIDAI identity. **PAN–Aadhaar linking is mandatory** — an unlinked PAN becomes *inoperative* (higher TDS, can't file/refund). |
| **HUF (Hindu Undivided Family)** | A separate assessee with its own PAN, taxed like an individual; used for family tax planning. |
| **Residential Status** | Decides what income is taxable in India. **Resident** (global income), **RNOR** (Resident but Not Ordinarily Resident), **Non‑Resident (NR)** (only India‑sourced income). Based on days of stay. |
| **Section 139(1)** | The provision that requires/allows filing the return by the due date. |
| **Nil Return** | A return filed when income is below the taxable limit — useful as income proof, for refunds, visas, loans, and to carry forward losses. |

## 2. The Five Heads of Income

1. **Salary** — employer‑employee income (basic, HRA, allowances, perquisites).
2. **House Property** — rental income (or notional rent) from owned property.
3. **Profits & Gains of Business or Profession (PGBP)** — business, trade, freelancing, consulting, F&O.
4. **Capital Gains** — profit on sale of capital assets (shares, MFs, property, gold).
5. **Income from Other Sources (IFOS)** — interest, dividends, gifts, lottery, residual income.

## 3. Tax Regimes

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Old Regime** | Higher slab rates but allows ~70+ deductions/exemptions (80C, 80D, HRA, LTA, home‑loan interest). |
| **New Regime (Sec 115BAC)** | **Default** since AY 2024‑25. Lower slab rates but **almost no deductions**. Best for those without large investments/loans. |
| **Standard Deduction** | Flat deduction from salary: **₹75,000** (new) / **₹50,000** (old). Salaried/pensioners only. |
| **Form 10‑IEA** | Filed by a person **with business/professional income** to **opt OUT** of the new regime (choose old). Not needed to stay in new. Salaried switch directly in the ITR each year. |
| **Default vs Opt‑in** | Do nothing → **new regime** applies. You must actively choose the old regime. |

### Tax Slabs — New Regime (FY 2025‑26)
| Income slab | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹4,00,000 | Nil |
| ₹4,00,001 – ₹8,00,000 | 5% |
| ₹8,00,001 – ₹12,00,000 | 10% |
| ₹12,00,001 – ₹16,00,000 | 15% |
| ₹16,00,001 – ₹20,00,000 | 20% |
| ₹20,00,001 – ₹24,00,000 | 25% |
| Above ₹24,00,000 | 30% |

*Basic exemption ₹4L. With the 87A rebate, income up to ₹12L is effectively tax‑free (₹12.75L for salaried after standard deduction).*

### Tax Slabs — Old Regime (individuals < 60 yrs)
| Income slab | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹2,50,000 | Nil |
| ₹2,50,001 – ₹5,00,000 | 5% |
| ₹5,00,001 – ₹10,00,000 | 20% |
| Above ₹10,00,000 | 30% |

*Higher basic exemption for seniors (₹3L for 60+, ₹5L for 80+).*

## 4. Core Computation Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Gross Total Income (GTI)** | Sum of income under all five heads, before Chapter VI‑A deductions. |
| **Total / Taxable Income** | GTI **minus** deductions. Tax is computed on this. |
| **Deduction** | A reduction from income (e.g., 80C). Reduces taxable income. |
| **Exemption** | Income not taxed at all (agricultural income, HRA portion, LTCG up to ₹1.25L). |
| **Rebate (Sec 87A)** | A reduction in **tax** for small taxpayers. New regime: up to **₹60,000** if total income ≤ **₹12L**. Old: up to **₹12,500** if income ≤ **₹5L**. *Not available against special‑rate income like STCG 111A / LTCG 112A (from AY 2026‑27).* |
| **Surcharge** | Extra tax on high incomes: 10% (>₹50L), 15% (>₹1Cr), 25% (>₹2Cr), 37% (>₹5Cr; capped at 25% under new regime). |
| **Health & Education Cess** | **4%** on (tax + surcharge). |
| **Marginal Relief** | Caps tax/surcharge so that crossing a threshold by a little doesn't raise tax by more than the extra income. |

## 5. Documents & Statements

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Form 16** | TDS certificate from employer for **salary** (Part A: TDS, Part B: salary breakup). |
| **Form 16A** | TDS certificate for **non‑salary** payments (interest, professional fees, rent). |
| **Form 26AS** | Consolidated tax statement: TDS/TCS, advance tax, self‑assessment tax, refunds. From TRACES. |
| **AIS (Annual Information Statement)** | Comprehensive statement of **all** reported transactions — income, interest, dividends, securities, SFT. Allows feedback. |
| **TIS (Taxpayer Information Summary)** | A **simplified, category‑wise summary** derived from the AIS, used for pre‑filling. |
| **Form 15G / 15H** | Self‑declarations to avoid TDS on interest if income is below the taxable limit (15H for seniors). |
| **Form 10E** | For claiming **Sec 89 relief** on salary arrears/advance. |
| **Form 67** | For claiming **Foreign Tax Credit** under a DTAA. |
| **SFT** | Statement of Financial Transactions — high‑value transactions reported by banks/registrars (large deposits, MF purchases, property). |

## 6. Tax Payment & Credit Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **TDS** | Tax Deducted at Source — withheld by payer (employer, bank) and deposited against your PAN. |
| **TCS** | Tax Collected at Source — collected by seller on certain sales (foreign remittance, car > ₹10L). |
| **Advance Tax** | Paid **during** the FY (installments) if total tax liability ≥ **₹10,000**. Due 15 Jun (15%), 15 Sep (45%), 15 Dec (75%), 15 Mar (100%). Presumptive taxpayers: 100% by **15 March**. |
| **Self‑Assessment Tax (SAT)** | Balance tax paid **at filing** (challan minor head **300**). |
| **Refund** | Excess tax returned by the department, with interest u/s 244A. |

## 7. Interest & Penalties

| Section | Trigger | Charge |
|---|---|---|
| **234A** | Filing return late | 1%/month on unpaid tax |
| **234B** | Advance tax unpaid / < 90% of liability | 1%/month |
| **234C** | Advance tax installments short/deferred | 1%/month per shortfall |
| **234F** | Late filing fee | ₹1,000 (income ≤ ₹5L) / ₹5,000 (> ₹5L) |
| **270A** | Under‑reporting / mis‑reporting income | 50% / 200% of tax |

## 8. Types of Returns (by timing)

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Original Return** | Filed on/before the due date u/s 139(1). |
| **Belated Return** | Filed after the due date, up to **31 Dec** of the AY (late fee u/s 234F). |
| **Revised Return** | Correction of an already‑filed return, up to **31 Dec** of the AY. |
| **Updated Return (ITR‑U)** | Declare missed income, up to **48 months** from end of AY (Budget 2025), with additional tax (25%–70%). |
| **Defective Return (139(9))** | Flagged as incomplete/inconsistent; must be corrected within the notice period. |
| **e‑Verification** | Mandatory final step (Aadhaar OTP / net banking / EVC) within **30 days**, else the return is invalid. |

## 9. Presumptive Taxation (declare a fixed % as income, no books needed)

| Section | For whom | Deemed income | Receipts/turnover limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **44AD** | Resident small **businesses** (not LLP) | **8%** of turnover (**6%** digital) | ≤ ₹2 Cr (₹3 Cr if cash ≤ 5%) |
| **44ADA** | Resident **professionals** (medical, legal, engineering, accountancy, technical consultancy, IT, etc.) | **50%** of gross receipts | ≤ ₹50 L (₹75 L if cash ≤ 5%) |
| **44AE** | **Goods transport** (≤ 10 vehicles) | Fixed amount per vehicle/month | — |

- No need to maintain books or get audited (if conditions met). Declaring **less** than the presumptive % → keep books + tax audit.
- **44AD 5‑year lock‑in:** opt out after opting in, and you're barred from 44AD for the next 5 years. **44ADA has no lock‑in** (choose year by year).
- Presumptive income **+ STCG/other disqualifiers** → file **ITR‑3**, not ITR‑4.

## 10. Capital Gains — Key Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Capital Asset** | Property of any kind: shares, MFs, land, building, gold (excludes stock‑in‑trade, personal effects). |
| **STCG (Short‑Term)** | Gain on asset held ≤ 12 m (listed equity/equity MF) or ≤ 24 m (property/unlisted shares/gold). |
| **LTCG (Long‑Term)** | Gain on asset held beyond the short‑term limit. |
| **Section 111A** | STCG on listed equity / equity MF (STT paid): **20%** (transfers on/after 23 Jul 2024; was 15%). |
| **Section 112A** | LTCG on listed equity / equity MF: **12.5%** above **₹1.25 L**/yr, **no indexation**. |
| **Section 112** | LTCG on other assets (property, gold, unlisted shares): **12.5%** without indexation (post 23 Jul 2024). |
| **Section 50AA** | "Specified mutual funds" (debt/liquid, ≤35% equity, bought after 1 Apr 2023): gains **always slab rate**, any holding period. |
| **Indexation** | Inflation‑adjusting cost (CII) to reduce LTCG. Largely **removed** from 23 Jul 2024 (limited option for pre‑2024 property). |
| **Grandfathering** | Equity bought **before 31 Jan 2018**: cost = higher of actual cost or FMV on 31 Jan 2018. Reported scrip‑wise in **Schedule 112A**. |
| **STT** | Securities Transaction Tax — paid on listed trades; a condition for 111A/112A rates. |

### Capital Gains Exemptions (reinvestment)
- **Sec 54** — LTCG on a residential house reinvested in another house.
- **Sec 54F** — LTCG on any asset reinvested in a residential house.
- **Sec 54EC** — LTCG on land/building invested in NHAI/REC bonds (up to ₹50L).

### Capital Loss Set‑off Rules
- **Short‑term capital loss (STCL)** → set off against **both** STCG and LTCG.
- **Long‑term capital loss (LTCL)** → set off **only** against LTCG.
- Unabsorbed losses **carry forward 8 years** — *only if the return is filed by the due date*.

## 11. Investment Taxation — Quick Reference

### Mutual Funds (by type)
| Fund type | Short‑term | Long‑term |
|---|---|---|
| **Equity** (≥65% equity) | ≤12 m → **20%** (111A) | >12 m → **12.5%** above ₹1.25L (112A) |
| **Debt / Liquid** (bought after 1 Apr 2023) | **Slab rate** (Sec 50AA) | **Slab rate** — no LTCG benefit, any holding |
| **Hybrid** | Depends on equity %: ≥65% → taxed as equity; ≤35% → as debt; in between → as per scheme rules |

*SIP note: each SIP installment is a separate purchase lot — its holding period is counted from that installment's date (FIFO on redemption).*

### Other Instruments
| Instrument | Taxation |
|---|---|
| **Dividends (shares/MF)** | Taxable at **slab rate** under IFOS. TDS @10% if dividend > **₹10,000**/yr (FY 25‑26 threshold). |
| **Bank/FD interest** | Slab rate. TDS @10% if interest > **₹50,000** (₹1,00,000 seniors) per bank (FY 25‑26). |
| **PPF** | **EEE** — investment (80C), interest, and maturity all tax‑free. |
| **EPF / VPF** | Tax‑free, **but** interest on employee contributions above **₹2.5 L/yr** is taxable. |
| **NPS** | 60% lump sum at maturity tax‑free; 40% annuity taxed at slab when received. Extra ₹50k via 80CCD(1B). |
| **Sovereign Gold Bond (SGB)** | 2.5% interest taxed at slab; **capital gain on maturity redemption is exempt** (taxable if sold in market). |
| **Physical gold / Gold ETF / fund** | Capital gains; physical gold > 24 m → LTCG 12.5%. (Gold fund/ETF rules vary by purchase date — verify.) |
| **REITs / InvITs** | Distributions split into dividend/interest/rental (slab) + return‑of‑capital (reduces cost). Units: 111A/112A like equity. |
| **Share buyback** | From 1 Oct 2024, proceeds taxed as **deemed dividend** (slab) in your hands; share cost becomes a capital loss. |
| **F&O / Intraday** | F&O = **non‑speculative business income**; intraday equity = **speculative business income**. Both → PGBP, ITR‑3. |

### Investor Tactics & Cross‑border
- **Tax‑gain harvesting** — book equity LTCG up to **₹1.25L/yr tax‑free** (sell & rebuy to reset cost).
- **Tax‑loss harvesting** — book STCL to offset STCG/LTCG before year‑end.
- **LRS / 20% TCS** — foreign remittances (US stocks, travel) above **₹10L/yr** attract **20% TCS** (creditable/refundable against your tax). Foreign shares are **unlisted** → LTCG after 24 m at 12.5%.
- **Schedule FA** — residents **must** disclose foreign shares, accounts, ESOPs of foreign companies, etc.

## 12. Loss Set‑off, Clubbing & Other Concepts

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Intra‑head set‑off** | Loss set off against income from another source under the **same** head. |
| **Inter‑head set‑off** | Remaining loss set off against income under **another** head (with restrictions). |
| **Carry‑forward** | Unabsorbed losses carried to future years (capital losses 8 yrs; business losses 8 yrs; house‑property loss 8 yrs). |
| **Clubbing of Income** | Income of spouse/minor child added to yours to prevent tax avoidance. |
| **DTAA** | Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement — avoids the same income being taxed twice; gives **Foreign Tax Credit** via Form 67. |
| **VDA (Sec 115BBH)** | Virtual Digital Assets (crypto/NFT) — flat **30%** tax + 1% TDS; **no loss set‑off**. Reported in **Schedule VDA**. |

## 13. Assessment, Notices & Audit

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Intimation u/s 143(1)** | Auto‑generated after filing — compares your computation with the department's; shows refund / demand / no change. |
| **Notice u/s 142(1)** | Inquiry before assessment — asks you to file a return or produce documents/details. |
| **Scrutiny u/s 143(2)** | Your return is picked for **detailed examination**. |
| **Notice u/s 148** | **Income escaping assessment** — reopening/reassessment of a past year. |
| **Rectification u/s 154** | Correcting a "mistake apparent from record" in an order/intimation. |
| **Tax Audit (Sec 44AB)** | Mandatory if business turnover > ₹1 Cr (₹10 Cr if cash ≤ 5%), or professional receipts > ₹50 L, or presumptive opt‑out with income above limit. Audit by a CA (Form 3CB/3CD). |
| **Books of Accounts (Sec 44AA)** | Specified professions / higher‑income businesses must maintain books. Presumptive filers are exempt. |
| **Faceless Assessment** | Assessments conducted electronically, without physical interface, via the National Faceless Assessment Centre. |
| **Condonation of Delay** | Request to the department to allow a late filing/verification for genuine hardship. |

## 15. Which ITR Form Should You File?

| Form | Who can file | Key conditions / limits |
|---|---|---|
| **ITR‑1 (Sahaj)** | Resident individual: **salary/pension**, **one** house, other sources, **LTCG 112A ≤ ₹1.25L**. | Income **≤ ₹50L**. No business, no STCG, no foreign assets, not a director. |
| **ITR‑2** | Individual/HUF: **salary + capital gains**, multiple houses, foreign income/assets, director, income **> ₹50L**. | **No** business/professional income. |
| **ITR‑3** | Individual/HUF with **business or professional income** (incl. **presumptive**) + anything else — capital gains, F&O, multiple properties. | The "catch‑all" for business/profession. Use if you have STCG **and** presumptive income. |
| **ITR‑4 (Sugam)** | Resident individual/HUF/firm (not LLP) with **presumptive income** (44AD/ADA/AE). | Income **≤ ₹50L**; LTCG 112A ≤ ₹1.25L allowed, but **NO STCG**, no foreign assets, no multiple properties, no F&O. |
| **ITR‑5** | Firms, LLPs, AOPs, BOIs. | Not for individuals. |
| **ITR‑6** | Companies (except those claiming Sec 11). | — |
| **ITR‑7** | Trusts, charities, political parties (Sec 139(4A)–(4F)). | — |

### Quick decision flow
- Salary + one house + small interest, ≤ ₹50L → **ITR‑1**
- Salary/CG/multiple houses/foreign, **no business** → **ITR‑2**
- **Presumptive**, simple, ≤ ₹50L, **no STCG** → **ITR‑4**
- **Any business/profession** with CG, F&O, > ₹50L, or STCG → **ITR‑3**

## 17. Common Chapter VI‑A Deductions (Old Regime mainly)

| Section | Deduction | Limit |
|---|---|---|
| **80C** | PF, PPF, ELSS, LIC, home‑loan principal, tuition | ₹1,50,000 |
| **80CCD(1B)** | Extra NPS contribution | ₹50,000 |
| **80CCD(2)** | Employer's NPS contribution | up to 14% of salary *(allowed even in new regime)* |
| **80D** | Health insurance premium | ₹25,000 (₹50,000 seniors) |
| **80E** | Education loan interest | No limit (8 yrs) |
| **80G** | Donations | 50%/100% as specified |
| **80GG** | Rent paid (when no HRA) | up to ₹60,000/yr |
| **80TTA** | Savings interest | ₹10,000 |
| **80TTB** | Interest income for seniors | ₹50,000 |
| **24(b)** | Home‑loan interest (self‑occupied) | ₹2,00,000 |

*Under the **new regime**, most of these are **not** available — chiefly only 80CCD(2) and 80JJAA.*

## 18. General Filing Steps

1. **Pick the regime** (old vs new) — compute both, choose lower tax.
2. **Choose the correct ITR form** (table above).
3. **Gather documents** — Form 16/16A, AIS, TIS, Form 26AS, bank/broker/MF statements, deduction proofs.
4. **Reconcile** your records with AIS/26AS; submit AIS feedback if anything is wrong.
5. **Fill schedules** — income heads, deductions, taxes paid.
6. **Compute tax**; pay any **self‑assessment tax** (challan 300).
7. **Submit** the return.
8. **e‑Verify within 30 days** (Aadhaar OTP / net banking / EVC).
9. Track **processing & refund** under e‑File → View Filed Returns; watch for the **143(1) intimation**.
